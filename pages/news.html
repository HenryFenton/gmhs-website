⚽ Sports Victory
December 7, 2024 Jamaica Gleaner

Garvey Maceo win daCosta Cup title in dramatic finale

A goal from Omarion O'Brian in the dying moments of the game sparked a dramatic end to the 2024 ISSA daCosta Cup final as Garvey Maceo clipped first-time finalist McGrath High 5-3 on penalties after a 1-1 draw in regulation time

Read Full Article

Garvey Maceo High School once again proved their dominance in Jamaican schoolboy football, claiming the 2024 DaCosta Cup title in a thrilling final. The squad, guided by their dedicated coaching staff, showcased exceptional teamwork, discipline and skill throughout the tournament.

The victory was greeted with jubilation across the school and the wider Clarendon community — a proud moment for every student, teacher and supporter who backed the team all season.

GMHS students delivered an outstanding performance in the 2024 CSEC examinations, with significant improvements in pass rates across Mathematics, English Language and Information Technology. Several students achieved Grade I passes across multiple subjects, reflecting the dedication of both learners and their teachers.

The school is immensely proud of every student who sat the examinations this year. Results represent the culmination of years of hard work, sacrifice and commitment by students and their families.

Garvey Maceo High School received a generous donation from our New York Past Students' Association towards continuous support for sporting activities. The contribution will fund equipment, kits and travel costs for the school's football and athletics programmes.

The school extends its deepest gratitude to every alumni member who contributed — your generosity keeps the GMHS sporting legacy alive across generations.

GMHS is pleased to welcome an expanded Information Technology department team for the 2024/25 academic year. The new appointment strengthens our capacity to deliver CSEC and CAPE IT, Web Design and Computer Networking to our growing student population.

The school continues to invest in technology education, recognising its importance in preparing students for Jamaica's digital economy and career pathways beyond secondary school.

Faculty Spotlight

GMHS student Olufemi Allen proudly received the Stewart's Hardware plaque for the Most Outstanding Art Piece alongside an NCB Gift Certificate, presented by Junior Atkinson, Head of the Art Department. The award recognises exceptional creative talent at the secondary school level.

Olufemi's achievement is a wonderful reminder that academic excellence at GMHS extends to the arts — and that our students continue to shine in every field they pursue.

GMHS alumnus Tyquendo Tracey helped Jamaica's 4×100m relay team to a silver medal in a new National Junior Record of 38.97 seconds at the 14th IAAF World Junior Championships in Barcelona, Spain. We are immensely proud of Tyquendo and the entire relay squad.

This achievement is a testament to the sporting tradition at Garvey Maceo — a school that has always believed in developing the whole student, on the track and in the classroom.
