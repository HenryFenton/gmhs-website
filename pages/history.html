A Legacy of Excellence

Named after two great heroes, Marcus Garvey and Antonio Maceo, Garvey Maceo High School has spent over four decades shaping the minds, character and futures of Clarendon's young people.

Our Founding Story

From a gift between nations to a cornerstone of Clarendon's community

🤝

A Gift from Cuba

Garvey Maceo High School opened its doors on May 19, 1980 from a generous gift from the government and people of Cuba, reflecting a bond of solidarity and shared commitment to education.

🌿

Rooted in Agriculture

Originally built as a Comprehensive High School with a major focus on agriculture, GMHS owns 90 acres of land and operates both a commercial farm and a tutorial farm. Students tend to pigs, goats and chickens, and plant and reap cash crops as part of their CXC Agricultural Science requirement.

📚

Academic Breadth

Alongside its agricultural roots, the school equips students across vocational and academic subjects, from Electrical Installation, Home Economics and Woodwork to the Arts, Sciences, Information Technology and Business.

📈

Decades of Growth

Since 1980 the student population has grown to three times its original size. Since 2001, School Improvement Plans (SIP) have guided the institution's continued structural and academic development.

Key Milestones

Over four decades of history, achievement and growth

1980

School Opens

Garvey Maceo High School officially opens on May 19, 1980, named after Marcus Garvey and Cuban hero Antonio Maceo. First Head Boy: Errol Roman. First Head Girl: Annette Powell.

1980

1991

Sports Champions

GMHS wins the Headley Cup and becomes all-island cricket champion, lifting the prestigious Spaulding Cup for the first time.

1991

1997

Headley Cup & Spaulding Cup

Another triumphant year as the school wins the Headley Cup again and shares the Spaulding Cup, cementing its reputation as a sporting powerhouse.

1997

2001

School Improvement Plans Begin

GMHS introduces School Improvement Plans (SIP) to oversee continued growth and development, structurally and academically.

2001

2007

DaCosta Cup & Ben Francis Cup

A landmark year for football as GMHS wins both the DaCosta Cup and the Ben Francis Cup, a double triumph celebrated across the school community.

2007

2008

All Together Sing Competition

GMHS enters the national All Together Sing competition for the first time and reaches the studio finals, with the school choir supported by CHASE Fund band equipment.

2008

2021

DaCosta Cup Champions Again

Garvey Maceo clinches the DaCosta Cup for the second time, proving that the school's football excellence endures across generations.

2021

Our Namesakes Marcus Garvey & Antonio Maceo

Named After Heroes

Garvey Maceo High School proudly carries the names of two towering figures in Caribbean and Latin American history, men whose courage, vision and belief in the dignity of their people continue to inspire every student who walks through our gates.

Their legacies are not just history, they are our daily call to excellence, resilience and purpose.

🇯🇲 Marcus Garvey Jamaican National Hero & Pan-Africanist
