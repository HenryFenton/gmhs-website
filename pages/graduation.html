CSEC & CAPE Requirements

The Caribbean Examinations Council qualifications that every GMHS student must achieve

CSEC 4th & 5th Form

Caribbean Secondary Education Certificate
  • Minimum of 5 CSEC subjects at Grade I, II or III to be eligible for graduation
  • English Language is mandatory, a pass is required for all certification pathways
  • Mathematics is strongly recommended and required for most 6th Form and tertiary programmes
  • Students may sit up to 10 subjects choose strategically based on career goals
  • School-Based Assessments (SBAs) must be submitted on time, late submissions affect final grades
CXC grades run from I (highest) to VI (fail). Grades I III are passes. Grades IV–VI do not count toward the graduation requirement.

CAPE 6th Form

Caribbean Advanced Proficiency Examination
  • Minimum of 2 CAPE Units required for a 6th Form completion certificate
  • Communication Studies and Caribbean Studies are compulsory for all 6th Form students
  • Each CAPE subject has Unit 1 (Lower 6th) and Unit 2 (Upper 6th) both must be completed for a full subject pass
  • The CAPE Associate Degree requires a minimum of 7 Units including Communication Studies and Caribbean Studies
  • Internal Assessments (IAs) carry significant weight, missing deadlines seriously impacts your final grade
CAPE results are recognised by UWI, UTech, NCU and all major Jamaican and Caribbean universities for direct entry.

Attendance & Conduct

Meeting these standards is essential for graduation eligibility at GMHS

📅

Minimum Attendance

Students must maintain a minimum of 80% attendance across the academic year to be eligible for graduation and to sit CXC examinations.

Absences due to illness must be supported by a medical certificate. Unexplained absences count against your attendance record.

Required minimum80%
📐

Code of Conduct

Students must demonstrate good conduct throughout their time at GMHS. Serious breaches of the school's code of conduct including persistent indiscipline, violence or dishonesty may result in withdrawal of graduation eligibility.

All incidents are recorded on the student's file and reviewed at graduation consideration meetings each term.

📝

SBA & IA Submission

School-Based Assessments (CSEC) and Internal Assessments (CAPE) are a required component of CXC examinations. Failure to submit SBAs/IAs will result in disqualification from that subject.

Deadlines are set by CXC and cannot be extended by the school. Students are responsible for tracking and meeting all submission dates.

💳

Financial Clearance

Students must have no outstanding school fees or levies to receive their graduation certificate and participate in the graduation ceremony. Parents are advised to settle any balances well in advance of the ceremony date.

Speak to the school bursar privately if you require a payment arrangement.

Community Service & HEART Trust

Building character and vocational readiness beyond the classroom

Giving Back to Your Community

In keeping with the values of Garvey Maceo High School and the wider Jamaican education system, students are required to complete a minimum of 40 hours of approved community service before graduation.

Community service develops leadership, empathy and civic responsibility values that the school and Jamaican society hold in high regard. Activities must be approved by the school and properly documented.

Additionally, eligible 5th and 6th Form students are encouraged to explore programmes offered through the HEART/NSTA Trust Jamaica's national skills training body which can complement your CXC qualifications with vocational certification.

1

Identify an Approved Activity

Community service must benefit others, some examples include church programmes, community clean-ups, tutoring, volunteering at health drives or supporting local organisations in Clarendon.

2

Get Prior Approval

Submit a proposal to the Guidance Counsellor or VP before starting. Unapproved activities will not count toward your requirement.

3

Log Your Hours

Keep a signed logbook or letter from your supervisor confirming hours completed. Submit this to the school office by the end of 5th Form.

🎓 HEART/NSTA Trust

Jamaica's national body for technical and vocational training and certification
Offers NVQ-J Level 1 to 3 qualifications alongside or after CXC
Relevant to students in IT, Business, Home Economics, Agriculture and Industrial Arts
HEART certification is widely recognised by Jamaican employers
Visit heart-nsta.gov.jm for programme listings
Speak to Mrs. Sharon Cascoe (Guidance Counsellor) for referrals
