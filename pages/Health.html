We Are Here for Your Child

Our Guidance Counsellor works directly with students across all year groups to support their emotional, social and academic wellbeing. All conversations are treated with the utmost confidentiality.

In Jamaica, many young people face pressures around exam performance, family challenges, peer relationships and identity. Our counselling service provides a safe, non-judgmental space where students can speak freely and receive professional support grounded in care and respect.

Parents and guardians are encouraged to reach out if they have concerns about their child's wellbeing early support makes a real difference.

Available Monday to Friday  |  7:40 am to 3:30 pm

Nutrition & Healthy Eating

Supporting students to fuel their bodies and minds throughout the school day

Good Food, Better Learning

Research consistently shows that students who eat well concentrate better, manage stress more effectively and achieve stronger academic results. At GMHS, we actively promote healthy eating habits drawn from Jamaica's rich food culture.

Our school canteen provides affordable, nutritious meals drawing on locally available ingredients. We encourage students to eat breakfast before school and choose balanced lunches that include staples like rice and peas, ground provisions, fresh fruit and vegetables.

The Government of Jamaica's School Feeding Programme supports eligible students and parents in financial need are encouraged to speak privately with the school office about accessing this support.

Start with Breakfast

Guidance for Parents & Guardians

How to support your child's health and wellbeing from home

Your Role Makes the Difference

Research from the Caribbean shows that parental involvement is one of the strongest predictors of a student's wellbeing and academic success. You don't need to have all the answers, you just need to be present, attentive and willing to communicate.

If you notice changes in your child's mood, appetite, sleep or school attendance, trust your instincts and reach out to us early. Early intervention is far more effective than waiting until a crisis develops.

Our Guidance Counsellor and school office are available to speak with you confidentially at any time during school hours.

1

Talk to Your Child Regularly

Ask open questions about their school day, friendships and how they're feeling.

2

Watch for Warning Signs

Withdrawal, loss of appetite, declining grades or unusual aggression can all signal deeper difficulties.

3

Contact the School Early

Call or email us and we will arrange a private consultation with our Guidance Counsellor.

4

Attend PTA Meetings

Our PTA meets three times per year, this is an excellent way to stay connected and raise concerns.

Have a Concern About Your Child?

Please don't hesitate to reach out. Our team is here to support every student and family, early conversations lead to better outcomes for everyone.
