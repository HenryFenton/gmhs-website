General

The current principal of Garvey Maceo High School is Mrs. Erica Ewbanks, who has led the school since 2010.

Uniform & Dress Code

Students must be appropriately attired for online school as dictated by the school's dress code. Students may wear their school uniform or a school shirt bearing the school logo.

Hairstyles must be simple no weaves, hair extensions or colour. No head ties or fancy headbands. No make-up or jewellery. Students in breach of these rules may be held in the waiting room until they are appropriately attired.

Uniform & Dress Code

The teacher has the right to hold students in the waiting room until they are appropriately attired. If a student refuses to comply after a warning, they will be denied access to the class and disciplinary action will be taken.

Uniform & Dress Code

The full uniform policy is available from the school office. Please contact us at garveymaceo.high.cla@moey.gov.jm to request a copy.

Online Learning

For general queries, school activity information and online class access, email garveymaceo.high.cla@moey.gov.jm. This inbox is monitored Monday to Friday between 7:40 am and 3:30 pm.

Online Learning

Students must display appropriate conduct at all times. There should be no unnecessary communication during classes. Designate a quiet space, ideally at a desk or table, free from noise, interruptions and distractions.

If you need to be excused, seek and receive permission first. Ask questions as directed by your teacher. All students must be online as scheduled and submit all Google Classroom assignments within the set deadlines.

Online Learning

Access codes for the LMS and Zoom are distributed by your form teacher at the start of each term. If you have not received yours, contact your form teacher or email the school at garveymaceo.high.cla@moey.gov.jm.

Online Learning

Curriculum documents for all form groups (1st to 3rd, 4th & 5th and 6th Form) are available to download on our Academics & Curriculum page.

Conduct & Discipline

GMHS expects all students to behave in a manner that reflects the school's core values, respect, integrity, responsibility and excellence. The full code of conduct is detailed in the student handbook, available from the school office.

Conduct & Discipline
